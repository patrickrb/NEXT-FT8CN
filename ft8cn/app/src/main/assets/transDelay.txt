Setting the transmission delay (in milliseconds) allows time for decoding after the previous listening cycle ends.
Setting a delay allows the app to know the other party's response from the previous cycle before transmitting, and react accordingly in the current cycle.
The delay setting depends on the device's processing capability - the delay must not be shorter than the time needed for decoding.
If the delay is too short, the app may not be able to react to signals from the previous cycle.
If the delay is too long, other stations may not be able to properly decode your signal.
In practice, since the radio has a response time after receiving the PTT command (FT8CN assumes 100 milliseconds), the actual audio signal transmission time is the transmission delay plus 100 milliseconds. For example, with a 500 ms transmission delay, the actual signal transmission starts at 500 + 100 = 600 milliseconds.
The actual FT8 signal cycle length is 12.64 seconds. It is recommended that the maximum delay not exceed 1.08 seconds.
